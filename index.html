About

The Data spAces for smaRt Energy (DARE) project aims to provide new, secure means for sharing data generated by IoT devices. DARE technology will enable IoT device owners to share data generated by their devices with 3rd party service providers. DARE integrates novel data semantics interoperability technologies, and security and access control solutions.

DARE will demonstrate the developed technologies through a smart energy management proof of concept. DARE will allow sharing of data generated by IoT devices located in real smart buildings, enabling training of AI models for anomaly detection and energy forecasting, as well as real time monitoring of energy equipment of smart buildings.

The main building block of DARE is W3C's Verifiable Credentials and Decentralized Identifiers. DARE will integrate these technologies into iSHARE framework in order to provide fine-grained access control. Data collected from IoT devices, will be properly encoded using JSON-LD and it will be provided through FIWARE orion context broker, using NSGI-LD API.

The DARE team

ExcID

ExcID is the project coordinator and holds the role of trust provider.

DomX

DomX holds the role of data provider and provides data from IoT devices installed in smart buildings.

Plegma

Plegma holds the role of data provider and provides data from IoT devices installed in smart buildings.

StreamOWL

StreamOWL holds the role of data consumer and uses the provided data to train AI models.

Gas Options

Gas Options holds the role of data consumer and uses the provided data to detect faults in gas installations.

Chthon

Chthon holds the role of data consumer and uses the provided data to perform energy demand forecasting.

DIH

The Internet of Things, Intelligent Systems, Data Engineering and Media DIH is a Digital Innovation Hub that provides support to the project.
